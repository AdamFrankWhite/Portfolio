ABOUT

Seeking a career-change, I am searching for an entry-level role, looking to begin my career in web development and programming.

Self-taught, my initial introduction into programming was learning Python. Having understood the core programming concepts, I found myself impatient to create something more web-based, and so I switched my focus over to JavaScript, along with HTML and CSS.

After a couple of months of learning, I began to build my first rudimentary web pages and web applications. The nature of programming - solving problems and constructing solutions - is inherently satisfying, enjoyable, and somewhat addictive!

Starting out, the never-ending acronyms and abstract technologies were somewhat overwhelming, however I gradually figured out a path of frameworks and libraries that I wanted to learn. I became increasingly more comfortable not knowing everything, and the learning itself became its own reward. The constant nature of learning is infectious, and I understand now, is part of the charm of programming.

Torn between wanting to learn everything, and needing to focus, I slowly absorbed the basics of a variety of tools including jQuery, Bootstrap and AJAX. Now, having learned the fundamentals, I am seeking my first front end role, where I can immerse myself in coding and learn from other developers.
