About

Seeking a career-change, I have taught myself the fundamentals of front-end programming.

My inital introduction into programming was learning Python. Having learned the core programming concepts, I found myself impatient to create something more web-based, and so I switched my focus over to JavaScript, along with HTML and CSS.

After a couple of months of learning, I began to build my first rudimentary web pages and web applications. The nature of programming - solving problems and constructing solutions - is inherently satisfying and enjoyable.

Starting out, the never-ending acronyms and abstract technologies was somewhat overwhelming boggling, however I gradually figured out a path of frameworks and libraries I wanted to learn. I became increasingly more comfortable not knowing everything, and the learning itself became addictive. Torn between wanting to learn everything, and needing to focus, I slowly absorbed the basics of a variety of tools including jQuery, Bootstrap and AJAX. The constant nature of learning is infectious, and I understand now, is part of the charm of programming.

I am searching for an entry-level role, beginning my career in web development and programming.
